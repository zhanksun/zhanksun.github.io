I am an associate professor of [Decision Analytics and Operations](https://www.cb.cityu.edu.hk/staff/zhanksun/) and the MBA program director in the College of Business, City University of Hong Kong. I received my bachelor's degree in Industrial Engineering from [Tsinghua University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tsinghua_University) in 2009, and my Ph.D. in Statistics and Operations Research from [UNC Chapel Hill](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_North_Carolina_at_Chapel_Hill) in 2014<a href="/files/PhD-Degree.pdf" style="color: inherit; text-decoration: none;">.</a> From 2014 to 2016, I was an Eyes High scholar at the Haskayne School of Business, during which I collaborated closely with [Alberta Health Services](https://www.albertahealthservices.ca/) and emergency departments in Alberta, Canada.

My research focuses on the modeling, analysis, and control of stochastic systems. I am particularly interested in applications arising from healthcare and smart-city operations, including (i) patient flow management in hospital emergency departments; (ii) emergency response management in the aftermath of mass-casualty events; and (iii) optimal charging strategies for battery swapping stations. 

I received the [College Research Excellence Awards](https://zhanksun.github.io/) in 2023, and was recognized for [Outstanding Teaching Performance](https://zhanksun.github.io/) (top 5% of teachers) at City University of Hong Kong in both 2023 and 2025.
